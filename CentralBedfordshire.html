                Local authority analysis: Central Bedfordshire

                Analysing the impact of the pandemic on children’s speech development

                Children entering school in the year after the first lockdown are showing a big increase in speech, language and communication (SLC) needs, according to analysis of DfE data. Find out how the numbers changed in Central Bedfordshire.

                In Central Bedfordshire the number of children needing support for speech, language and communication in Year 1 rose from 105 in 2015/16 to 185 in 2021/22.

                The number of children needing support for speech, language and communication in Year 1 in 2021/22 dropped by -5.61% compared to the previous academic year. This is below the the national rise of 9.81%. The authority ranks 10 in the region out of 11 authorities.
